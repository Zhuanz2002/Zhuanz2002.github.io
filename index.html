01 / ONSIGHT

OnSight

On-device vision-language support for contextual and privacy-preserving navigation in blind mobility.

OnSight combines first-person camera input, GPS and route context, vision-language inference, temporal filtering, and concise spoken feedback. The goal is a mature app that helps blind and low-vision users navigate to destinations, explore nearby environments, and find specific objects without replacing established mobility tools or user judgment.

01 · System

A local-first, cloud-capable system

Rear-camera frames and CoreLocation data feed route reasoning and vision-language inference. Local mode prioritizes privacy and predictable latency; an explicit cloud mode can support richer models when users choose connectivity and data sharing.

02 · Evidence

Evaluated in real-world mobility tasks

The paper reports a counterbalanced study with 13 blind adults using an iPhone 16 Pro across four outdoor route segments and indoor exploration tasks, comparing on-device and cloud-backed conditions.

03 · Next

From prototype to downloadable product

The roadmap separates three user goals—Navigate, Explore Surroundings, and Find an Object—while improving accessibility, uncertainty handling, privacy controls, testing, and long-session reliability.

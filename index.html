About

Engineering autonomy from hardware to intelligence.

I am pursuing an M.S. in Electrical & Computer Engineering at Boston University. My work sits at the boundary of embedded systems and intelligent autonomy: connecting sensors, algorithms, controllers, and physical machines into dependable end-to-end systems.

Previously, I earned my B.S. in Electrical Engineering at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, where I built autonomous robots and automated manufacturing systems. I am especially interested in safety-critical autonomy and technology that expands human capability.
